## Key Coverage Options to Consider

When selecting a building insurance policy, it's essential to pay close attention to specific coverage options that can provide comprehensive protection for your property. Some key coverage options to consider include:

### 1. Building Coverage

This policy extends to cover your building and any additional structures on your property, such as storage facilities, garages, retaining walls, and fences. It provides financial protection in the event of damage or loss due to covered perils.

### 2. Loss of Income

In the event of a shutdown or closure of your building due to an accident or mishap, this provision covers the loss of income that you may incur. It helps ensure that your business operations can continue despite unexpected disruptions.

### 3. Comprehensive General Liability

Protect yourself against legal liabilities arising from personal injury, bodily injury, property damage, or advertising injury to third parties. This coverage offers financial protection in the face of potential lawsuits or legal claims.

### 4. Data Compromise/Identity Restoration

In today's digital age, data breaches and identity theft are significant concerns for businesses. This coverage provides support in the event of a data breach, helping you recover and restore your operations after a security incident.

### 5. Equipment Failure

Covering damages to your building resulting from mechanical equipment failure, this policy safeguards against unexpected repair costs due to equipment malfunctions. It includes coverage for incidents like steam boiler explosions that can damage your property.

### 6. Building Ordinance and Law

This provision covers the costs associated with complying with municipal regulations or ordinances following damage to your building. It ensures that you are protected against additional expenses and loss of value resulting from regulatory requirements.
